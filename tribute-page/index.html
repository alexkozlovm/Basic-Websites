Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi

The man who's philosophy of nonviolence inspired civil rights leaders around the world.

Here's a time line of Gandhi's life

  • 1869 - Born in Porbandar, British India
  • 1888 - Gandhi travels to London to study law
  • 1893 - Gandhi goes to South Africa to work as a lawyer and becomes involved in civil rights activism
  • 1915 - Returns to India and becomes a leader in the Indian National Congress
  • 1919- Leads the nonviolent protests against the Rowlatt Act and the Jallianwala Bagh massacre
  • 1920- Launches the Non-Cooperation Movement, advocating for nonviolent resistance against British rule
  • 1930- Leads the Salt March, a 240-mile protest against the British salt tax
  • 1931- Attends the Round Table Conference in London as the sole representative of the Indian National Congress
  • 1942- Launches the Quit India Movement, demanding an end to British rule in India
  • 1942-1944- Imprisoned by the British during the Quit India Movement
  • 1947- India gains independence from British rule
  • 1948- Assassinated in New Delhi by Nathuram Godse, a Hindu nationalist

“The future depends on what we do in the present.” -Gandhi
